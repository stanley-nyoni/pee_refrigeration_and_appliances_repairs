Professional Stove & Oven Repairs

Is your stove not heating? Oven temperature inaccurate? Gas burner not lighting? Our certified technicians specialize in repairing all types of cooking appliances - from electric and gas stoves to built-in ovens and cooktops.

Common Stove & Oven Problems We Fix

  • Burner not heating: Electric element or gas burner not working
  • Uneven heating: Hot spots or cold spots in oven
  • Temperature inaccuracy: Oven not reaching set temperature
  • Igniter issues: Gas burner not lighting or staying lit
  • Control panel problems: Buttons, knobs, or display not working
  • Door issues: Oven door not closing or sealing properly
  • Broken glass: Cooktop or oven door glass damage
  • Fan problems: Oven fan not working or making noise
  • Clock/timer issues: Digital display or timer malfunction
  • Spark module failure: Gas ignition system problems

Oven Temperature Calibration

Temperature Accuracy

Most ovens can drift 10-25°C from set temperature over time. We use professional oven thermometers to calibrate your oven to within ±5°C accuracy for perfect cooking results.

Safety First - Gas Appliance Safety

Gas Safety Warning

If you smell gas: 1) Turn off gas supply, 2) Open windows, 3) Don't use electrical switches, 4) Evacuate if smell is strong, 5) Call us immediately for emergency service. Never attempt DIY repairs on gas appliances.

Maintenance Tips for Cooking Appliances

Daily Maintenance
  • Wipe spills immediately
  • Clean burner caps and grates
  • Check for proper flame (gas)
Weekly Maintenance
  • Clean oven interior
  • Wipe control panel
  • Clean under burners
Monthly Maintenance
  • Clean oven fan (if accessible)
  • Check door seals
  • Test all burners/elements
Annual Professional Service
  • Calibrate oven temperature
  • Check electrical connections
  • Inspect gas lines (gas appliances)
  • Test safety features

Energy Saving Tips

1) Use the right size burner for your pot - matching burner to pot size saves 40% energy. 2) Keep oven door closed while cooking - each opening loses 20% heat. 3) Use residual heat - turn off electric elements a few minutes early. 4) Keep reflectors clean on electric stoves.
