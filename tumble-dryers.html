Professional Tumble Dryer Repairs

Is your tumble dryer not heating? Taking too long to dry? Making strange noises? Our certified technicians specialize in repairing all types of tumble dryers - from vented and condenser dryers to heat pump and gas dryers.

Common Tumble Dryer Problems We Fix

  • Not heating: Dryer running but not producing heat
  • Not starting: Dryer not turning on or starting
  • Not spinning: Drum not rotating or rotating slowly
  • Overheating: Dryer getting too hot or cutting out
  • Long drying times: Clothes taking too long to dry
  • Strange noises: Squealing, grinding, or banging sounds
  • Error codes: Display showing error messages
  • Door issues: Door not closing or latching properly
  • Lint problems: Excessive lint or poor venting
  • Timer issues: Timer not advancing or stopping early

Types of Tumble Dryers We Repair

Vented Dryers

Require external venting to expel moist air

Features: Most common, requires vent hose

Condenser Dryers

Condense moisture into water container

Features: No external venting required

Heat Pump Dryers

Energy efficient with heat recovery system

Features: Most energy efficient, gentle drying

Gas Dryers

Use natural gas or LP gas for heating

Features: Lower operating costs, requires gas connection

Safety Considerations

Important Safety Information

Tumble dryers are a common cause of house fires. Regular maintenance and proper installation are essential. Never overload the dryer, always clean the lint filter after each use, and ensure proper venting.

Maintenance Checklist

After Every Use
  • Clean lint filter
  • Wipe door seal
  • Check for foreign objects
Monthly Maintenance
  • Clean vent hose
  • Check exterior vent
  • Inspect power cord
  • Clean moisture sensor
Annual Professional Service
  • Clean internal ducting
  • Check heating element
  • Test thermostats
  • Lubricate moving parts
  • Check belt tension
Vented Dryer Specific
  • Check vent hose for kinks
  • Ensure vent length < 6m
  • Clean exterior vent flap
  • Check for bird nests

Energy Saving Tips

1) Clean the lint filter after every load - a clogged filter increases drying time by 30%. 2) Don't overload the dryer - clothes need space to tumble. 3) Use moisture sensor settings if available. 4) Dry similar fabrics together for even drying.
